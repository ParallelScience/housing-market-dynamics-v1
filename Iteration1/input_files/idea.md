**Title: Structural Identification of Housing Market Shocks via Sign-Restricted VAR**

This research employs a Structural Vector Autoregression (SVAR) model with sign restrictions to decompose volatility in the U.S. housing market into exogenous demand-side and supply-side shocks from 1987 to 2024. By imposing theoretical constraints on the impulse response functions—specifically identifying 'Demand Shocks' where `CSUSHPINSA` and `HOUST` move in the same direction, and 'Supply Shocks' where they move in opposition—the model isolates the latent drivers of price discovery. The framework treats the `MORTGAGE30US` (monthly averaged) as an exogenous policy shock variable, allowing for the quantification of how interest rate volatility filters through to price adjustments versus construction intent without the overfitting risks of regime-partitioning. This approach circumvents the need for external macroeconomic variables by relying on the internal co-movement dynamics of the FRED series to identify structural shifts. The resulting impulse responses characterize the duration and intensity of the market's response to financing costs, providing a robust measure of how demand-led cycles diverge from supply-constrained equilibria.
